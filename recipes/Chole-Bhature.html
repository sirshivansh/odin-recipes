Chole Bhature

Description

Chole Bhature is a popular North Indian dish consisting of spicy chickpea curry (chole) served with deep-fried bread (bhature). The chickpea curry is made with a blend of aromatic spices, tomatoes, and onions, while the bhature is made from all-purpose flour and yogurt, resulting in a soft and fluffy texture. This dish is often enjoyed as a hearty breakfast or lunch and is typically accompanied by pickles, onions, and yogurt.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups chickpeas (soaked overnight)
  • 3 large tomatoes (pureed)
  • 2 large onions (finely chopped)
  • 2 green chilies (slit)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 teaspoons chole masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)
  • For Bhature:
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • Oil for deep frying

Steps:

  1. Cook the soaked chickpeas in a pressure cooker until soft. Set aside.
  2. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add finely chopped onions and saut√© until golden brown.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for another minute.
  5. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, and chole masala. Mix well.
  6. Add the pureed tomatoes and cook until the oil separates from the masala.
  7. Add the cooked chickpeas along with some water to achieve the desired consistency. Simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  8. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  9. For Bhature: In a mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, yogurt, sugar, and baking powder. Knead into a soft dough using water as needed. Let it rest for 1 hour.
  10. Divide the dough into small balls and roll them out into circles.
  11. Heat oil in a deep frying pan. Fry the rolled-out dough until they puff up and turn golden brown on both sides.
  12. Serve the hot chole with bhature, along with pickles and yogurt.
